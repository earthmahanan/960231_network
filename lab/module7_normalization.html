Why bother normalizing? Edgar F. Codd introduced normal forms in the early 1970s specifically to kill three anomalies — insertion, update, and deletion anomalies — that show up when one table is trying to describe more than one "fact" at a time. Every normal form you'll use today (1NF → 5NF) is just a stronger rule for making sure a table stores exactly one fact about exactly one thing.
